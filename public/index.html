How to Play
The Board

Six categories, five clues each. Dollar values increase with difficulty — $200 is a gimme, $1000 will hurt. Click any tile to open a clue. You control the board until someone else answers correctly.

Buzzing In

Read the clue, then hit BUZZ IN (or press Space) when you know it. Your two AI opponents are also reading — they'll buzz in on their own after a delay that depends on their speed and confidence. If you wait too long the window closes and the board moves on.

Answering

Answers are spoken — your microphone opens automatically after you buzz. Just say your answer naturally; no need to phrase it as a question. You have a few seconds. The AI judge handles spelling variants, nicknames, and speech-recognition mishearings.

Daily Double

Two tiles on the board are hidden Daily Doubles. When you land on one, you wager any amount up to your current score (minimum $5). Only you can answer — the AI opponents sit this one out.

Double Jeopardy

After the first board is cleared the dollar values double and a fresh set of categories is generated. The player in last place picks first.

Final Jeopardy

One category, one clue, 30 seconds. Everyone wagers blind before the clue is revealed. You answer by voice; the AI opponents answer silently. Scores update one by one for maximum drama.

AI Opponents

Each opponent has a different accuracy rating and buzz speed. Higher-dollar clues trip them up more often. They can also be wrong — if they miss, the clue stays open for others to steal.
